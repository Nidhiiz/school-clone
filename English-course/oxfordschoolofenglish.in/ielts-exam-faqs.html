IELTS Exam FAQS

IELTS Exam FAQS

Here are answers to frequently asked questions to help you understand the exam format, requirements, and registration process.



Frequently Asked Questions

The International English Language Testing System (IELTS) is a globally recognised English language proficiency test. It assesses the ability of non-native English speakers to communicate effectively in academic, professional, or everyday settings. The IDP manages IELTS in India. It evaluates four key language skills: Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking. Universities, employers, immigration authorities, and professional bodies in the UK, USA, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand widely accept IELTS scores. If you're looking for IELTS coaching in Delhi NCR, the Oxford School of English is the place to go.

Most universities require a band score of 6.5 or higher. Immigration and work requirements vary by country. Some institutions or employers may demand higher scores in specific sections. To avoid confusion, always confirm the required score with your employer, university, or immigration office before starting your IELTS coaching.

The IELTS exam lasts 2 hours and 45 minutes, divided into four sections:
  • Listening – 30 minutes
  • Reading – 60 minutes
  • Writing – 60 minutes
  • Speaking – 11–14 minutes

    • Each section evaluates different language skills.

    What is the IELTS General Exam Pattern?
    Sections:

    IELTS General Training Exam Pattern

    • Listening (30 minutes)
      • Format: 4 sections, 40 questions
      • Audio played once only
      • Additional 10 minutes to transfer answers
      • Question types:
        • Multiple choice
        • Fill in the blanks
        • Matching
        • Map/diagram labelling
    • Reading (60 minutes)
      • Format: 3 sections, 40 questions
      • Includes everyday texts, workplace documents, and longer articles
      • Question types:
        • True/False/Not Given
        • Multiple choice
        • Matching headings
        • Sentence & summary completion
    • Writing (60 minutes)
      • Task 1: Write a formal/semi-formal/informal letter (150 words, 20 mins)
      • Task 2: Write an essay on a general topic (250 words, 40 mins)
    • Speaking (11–14 minutes)
      • Part 1: Introduction & personal questions
      • Part 2: Cue card (1-minute prep + 2-minute talk)
      • Part 3: Follow-up discussion

    IELTS Academic Exam Pattern

    Total Duration: 2 hours 45 minutes

    • Listening (30 minutes)
      • Same as the General test
      • Includes lectures and academic discussions
    • Reading (60 minutes)
      • 3 academic passages from books, journals, or newspapers
      • Question types:
        • True/False/Not Given
        • Yes/No/Not Given
        • Matching
        • Diagram/table completion
    • Writing (60 minutes)
      • Task 1: Describe visual data (graph, chart, map, process)
      • Task 2: Write an academic essay on an issue or opinion
    • Speaking (11–14 minutes)
      • Format same as General test
      • Involves an introduction, cue card response, and extended discussion

    IELTS Test Formats

    • Computer-Based Test: Conducted at official test centers
    • Paper-Based Test: Handwritten format at designated centers

    IELTS Online (at home) – Only available for the Academic version

    • Identical format and scoring
    • Video call speaking test
    • AI + human proctoring
    • Results in 6–8 days

    Technical Requirements for IELTS Online

    • Device: Desktop/laptop only
    • OS: Windows 10 or latest macOS
    • Stable broadband, webcam, microphone
    • No tablets, phones, virtual backgrounds, or multiple screens

    Registration Steps

    • Check test dates on the official IELTS website
    • Register and pay
    • Receive system check and login details
    • Take the test at home
    • Get results online

    Register on the IDP official website or through authorised centres. Oxford School of English provides complete assistance for IELTS registration to all enrolled students.
