Types of IELTS Exam

IELTS Academic

This test suits students planning to study abroad in universities or professional institutions. It assesses your ability to understand and communicate in an academic setting, focusing on complex reading passages and formal writing tasks.

IELTS General Training

This test is for individuals migrating to English-speaking countries for work, residency, or general communication. It evaluates the practical language skills needed for everyday situations, workplace interactions, and social environments.

The IELTS Exam Pattern – Academic and General

4 Sections: Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking |
Duration: 2 Hrs 45 Mins

  • The Listening Section
    • Common to both formats
    • Lasts 30 minutes and features four recordings with 40 questions, including:
      • Multiple choice
      • Matching
      • Sentence completion
  • The Reading Section
    • Lasts 60 minutes and differs between the two formats:
    • Academic IELTS:
      • Includes three long passages from books, journals, or newspapers.
      • Features 40 questions covering:
        • Multiple choice
        • Matching headings
        • Short-answer tasks

    • General Training IELTS:
      • Includes shorter, more practical texts sourced from advertisements, notices, and workplace documents.
      • Uses similar question types as Academic.

  • The Writing Section
    • Lasts 60 minutes and varies significantly:

    • Academic IELTS:
      • Test-takers complete two tasks:
        • Task 1: Describe a graph, table, chart, or diagram
        • Task 2: Write an essay

    • General Training IELTS:
      • Task 1: Write a letter (formal, semi-formal, or informal)
      • Task 2: Write an essay on a general topic

  • The Speaking Section
    • Common to both formats
    • Lasts 11–14 minutes
    • Includes a face-to-face interview with three parts:
      • An introduction and general questions
      • A cue card task
      • A discussion based on the topic

Which IELTS Test Should You Take?

Which IELTS Test Should You Take?

Choose your IELTS exam based on your study, work, or migration goals. If you plan to enrol in a university, you need the IELTS Academic test. If you're applying for a job or immigration, you may require the IELTS General Training test. If unsure, consult your employer, university, or immigration authority before enrolling in IELTS coaching.
